# Indigo Gem Haskap (Lonicera caerulea)

---

## Overview
Indigo Gem is one of the most widely referenced commercial haskap cultivars and is often used as a **benchmark standard** in cold-climate berry production systems.

It is valued for reliability, consistent yields, and balanced fruit quality, making it a reference point for comparing newer cultivars such as Aurora.

Within Mori Mountain systems, Indigo Gem functions as a **baseline cultivar for performance comparison**.

---

## Taxonomy
- Family: Caprifoliaceae
- Genus: Lonicera
- Species: Lonicera caerulea
- Cultivar: Indigo Gem

---

## History
Indigo Gem was developed through Canadian breeding programs focused on establishing commercially viable haskap production in cold climates.

It became widely adopted due to its:
- predictable performance
- consistent yields
- suitability as a comparison standard in research and orchard trials

It is often treated as a “control cultivar” in haskap evaluation studies.

---

## Genetics
- Mixed Lonicera caerulea breeding background (Russian + Japanese germplasm lineage)
- Selected traits:
  - stable fruit production
  - moderate berry size
  - consistent ripening window
- Used as a **reference genotype** in cultivar comparisons

---

## Cultivation
- Growth habit: Upright, multi-stem shrub
- Vigor: Moderate
- Yield: Consistent but not maximal
- Pruning: Responds well to renewal pruning
- Orchard role: dependable backbone cultivar

---

## Climate & Hardiness
- USDA Zones: 2–4
- Strong cold hardiness
- Reliable flowering under variable spring conditions
- Stable in marginal climates compared to more sensitive selections

---

## Soil Biology
- Prefers slightly acidic soils (pH ~5.5–7)
- Performs well in a wide range of soil types
- Responsive to:
  - organic matter enrichment
  - mycorrhizal soil systems
  - compost-based fertility
- Suitable for Mori Mountain biochar and fungal inoculation trials

---

## Propagation
- Softwood cuttings (primary method)
- Tissue culture propagation for commercial scaling
- Maintains genetic consistency well under clonal propagation

## Phenology

- Overlaps well with other cultivars for pollination compatibility
- Predictable fruit ripening window in early summer
