# Honey Bee Haskap (Lonicera caerulea)

---

## Overview
Honey Bee is a commercial haskap cultivar selected for productivity, ease of pollination compatibility, and consistent fruit set. It is commonly used as a companion pollinator variety for other haskaps such as Aurora.

It is considered a foundational “pollination anchor” cultivar in modern haskap orchards.

---

## Taxonomy
- Family: Caprifoliaceae
- Genus: Lonicera
- Species: Lonicera caerulea
- Cultivar: Honey Bee

---

## History
Honey Bee was developed as part of Canadian haskap breeding programs focused on improving cross-pollination efficiency in commercial orchards. It was not primarily selected for flavor superiority, but for its role in stabilizing fruit production across mixed cultivar plantings.

It functions as a “system support plant” in orchard design.

---

## Genetics
- Mixed Lonicera caerulea breeding lines (Russian/Japanese ancestry)
- Selected traits:
  - strong pollen production
  - reliable bloom overlap with major cultivars
  - consistent flowering timing
- Genetically important as a **pollination bridge cultivar**

---

## Cultivation
- Growth habit: Upright shrub
- Vigor: Moderate to strong
- Flowering: Highly reliable
- Yield: Moderate fruit production (secondary to pollination role)
- Pruning: Responds well to renewal pruning

---

## Climate & Hardiness
- USDA Zones: 2–4
- Very cold hardy
- Stable flowering in cold spring conditions
- Less sensitive than some cultivars to early frost swings

---

## Soil Biology
- Prefers slightly acidic soils (pH ~5.5–7)
- Strong mycorrhizal relationships
- Responds well to:
  - organic mulch
  - fungal-rich soil systems
  - composted organic matter
- Compatible with Mori Mountain biochar inoculation trials

---

## Propagation
- Softwood cuttings (preferred)
- Tissue culture (commercial propagation)
- Nursery cloning for consistency
- Seed propagation not used for cultivar fidelity

---

## Phenology
- Mid-season flowering relative to other haskaps
- Overlaps bloom window with Aurora (critical trait)
- Fruit ripens early summer in cold climates

---

## Ecological Role
- Major pollination support cultivar
- High nectar and pollen availability
- Stabilizes orchard yield in mixed plantings
- Supports early-season pollinators

---

## Pest & Disease Profile
- Low disease pressure
- Similar resilience profile to Aurora
- Primary limitation: not fruit-quality optimized, but system-role optimized
