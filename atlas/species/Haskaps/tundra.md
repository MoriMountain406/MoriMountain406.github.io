# Tundra Haskap (Lonicera caerulea)

---

## Overview
Tundra is an early widely-distributed haskap cultivar used in cold-climate orchard systems. It is valued for reliability, early adoption history, and strong compatibility with other major cultivars.

Within Mori Mountain systems, Tundra serves as a **legacy-compatible cultivar** that represents earlier breeding stability before modern high-yield selections.

---

## Taxonomy
- Family: Caprifoliaceae
- Genus: Lonicera
- Species: Lonicera caerulea
- Cultivar: Tundra

---

## History
Tundra was part of early Canadian haskap development programs aimed at introducing edible blue honeysuckle to commercial agriculture.

It is considered one of the foundational cultivars that helped establish haskaps as a viable cold-climate crop in North America.

---

## Genetics
- Early breeding selection from Lonicera caerulea germplasm
- Traits selected for:
  - cold hardiness
  - reliable fruiting
  - basic commercial viability
- Represents earlier-stage genetic improvement compared to Aurora-class cultivars

---

## Cultivation
- Growth habit: Upright shrub
- Vigor: Moderate
- Yield: Stable but not optimized for modern high-density orchards
- Pruning: Standard renewal pruning practices apply
- Orchard role: baseline cultivar for hardy systems

---

## Climate & Hardiness
- USDA Zones: 2–4
- Excellent cold tolerance
- Reliable in marginal climates
- Strong dormancy response

---

## Soil Biology
- Prefers slightly acidic soils (pH ~5.5–7)
- Adaptable to a wide range of soil conditions
- Responds well to:
  - organic matter
  - fungal soil development
  - biochar amendments (experimental)

---

## Propagation
- Softwood cuttings (primary method)
- Tissue culture (possible in commercial systems)
- Stable clonal propagation

---

## Phenology
- Early flowering cultivar
- Fruit ripens in early summer
- Overlaps bloom window with other cultivars (important for orchard systems)

---

## Ecological Role
- Early pollinator resource
- Supports early-season bee activity
- Moderate ecological value in hedgerow systems

---

## Pest & Disease Profile
- Generally low pest pressure
- Stable across environments
- No major cultivar-specific vulnerabilities noted in general cultivation literature
